Sony are Killing Games

14th July 2026

I created this website today. There is a big reason for this as breifly explained in the welcome section.

On the 1st July 2026, Sony published a blog post on their official website titled "Physical disc production ending in January 2028 for new games releasing on PlayStation consoles". As of me typing this, the post has 1,233 likes and 9,058 comments. Clearly people are not happy. Here is a link to the original post: Physical disc production ending in January 2028 for new games releasing on PlayStation consoles But in case the post is deleted, here is the entire post in text format:

"As consumer preferences and the broader entertainment industry continue to shift away from physical discs to digital, physical game disc production for all new games releasing on PlayStation consoles will be discontinued starting January 2028. Following this date, new games will be available on PlayStation Store and at retailers in digital formats only. This transition has no impact on games that already released, or will be releasing, prior to January 2028 in disc format. This is a natural direction for Sony Interactive Entertainment to adapt to consumer trends as the general preference for digital media significantly outpaces physical discs. This transition will enable us to align more closely with how most of our community prefers to access and play games today. We’ll continue to prioritize our resources to drive innovation in how players can access games and provide choices as to where players prefer to purchase new games, whether that’s at retailers or PlayStation Store. We remain committed to delivering a world-class gaming experience to our fans and we thank you for your continued support. For important updates on PlayStation Store for PS3 and PS Vita announced today, please click here."

What. the. Fuck?

Very recently, Sony announced that over 500 movies and tv shows are set to be removed from PlayStation users on the 1st of September 2026 due to an expiring licensing agreement with StudioCanal. Users will not be recieving refunds for any of the removed content.

The reason I bring that up is to show that content that you buy digitally is able to be taken away from you at any moment and you will not recieve any refunds or other compensation. So then why are we being forced by Sony to only buy games digitally from 2028 onwards? What happens if your PlayStation account is wrongfully banned (which happens with digital accounts much more often than people realise) and all of your purchases on that account are gone forever with no refunds? What happens if you can't log in to your account for 3 and a half years or simply don't have time in your day-to-day life? That last question sound random? Well look at this from Sony's official Terms of Service:

"21.2 If you have not used your Account for at least 36 months we may take steps to close it. In that case, we will contact you via the email address registered to your Account and give you 6 months to either log in to your Account or contact us and tell us to keep your Account open. 21.3 After your Account is closed, you will not be able to access the PlayStation Online Services or use the Digital Products purchased with that Account. Account closure is irreversible.

You might sit there and think that this is an unlikely scenario and that 3 and a half years is reasonable. I would disagree. I know from my own personal experience that I have plenty of accounts on various websites, platforms, etc that I have not accessed in years. Although most of these, I have not spent money on and have no important stuff on them, some of them I do. It's the second term I quoted that I take most issue with. If an account is closed, the owner recieves no refunds for their purchases. I mean think about that for a second. Really? And your account is unable to be reopened according to Sony themselves. If this happens, it will be as if you never existed to begin with in PlayStation's eyes.

My personal plan going forward in terms of gaming is as follows:

  1. I will not be buying any digital games on any console from this point forward.
  2. I will not be buying any new consoles post PS5 and Xbox Series X (unless they still allow physical games like the Nintendo Switch 2 still does)
  3. I will be fully moving to PC and "retro" consoles only (Every console pre-PS6 in this case unless, again, if it's only PlayStation that move to digital only)
  4. I will be predominately pirating new releases on PC because if we no longer own the games we buy, then why would I bother buying them?
  5. I will likely stop playing games in general given how awful the modern gaming industry is and how it only seems to be getting worse with every passing day but the day that I turn away from this hobby that I've enjoyed for so much of my life is hopefully still a long way away
  6. I will continue to buy indie releases on PC through GameJolt, Steam, itch.io, etc. as long as these websites continue to be consumer friendly and actually allow us to keep games we've bought forever (love you Steam)
  7. I will also begin copying my digital console games to discs on PS3 and Xbox 360 as a backup and hopefully with newer consoles too if that ever becomes easily doable. I will also be buying physical albums and movies/tv shows on disc for my favourites to ensure that I actually own them for as long as the hardware survives
